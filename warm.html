My Story

Why I Teach Korean

In March 2025, I walked away from my business. I was completely burnt out. For the first time in my life, I just started traveling. As I wandered through different countries, I met incredible people who were genuinely curious about Korea.

It wasn't just K-Pop or K-Dramas. They wanted to understand the language itself, the culture, the way Koreans think. They asked questions I'd never deeply considered:

"Why do Koreans always ask about age?"

"Why does the verb come at the very end?"

"Why do you write addresses backwards?"

As I explained these things, something clicked. This is what I'm meant to do.

Before teaching Korean, I ran a self-directed learning coaching center in Korea for four years. At my peak, I was ranked 170th out of 450,000 tutors on Korea's largest tutoring platform — the top 0.03%.

"I didn't get there because I'm smarter than others.
I got there because I built systems."

The philosophy behind Fly Korean

My personal goal is for you to eventually not need me. A great tutor is someone who makes themselves unnecessary. My mission is to build a strong system so that you can fly on your own.

Teaching Philosophy

Three Pillars of Korean Mastery

Pronunciation First

Most teachers start with grammar. I start with pronunciation. Even with 10 words, if you pronounce them right, Koreans will understand and be impressed. Korean is forgiving on grammar but unforgiving on sounds.

The Three Consonant Levels

가 Plain — soft, relaxed
카 Aspirated — strong air
까 Tensed — tight, no air

Sentence Logic

Understand why Korean sentences work the way they do, not just memorize patterns. Learn to create your own sentences, not repeat textbook phrases.

Word Order Comparison

English thinks: action first

I bought a bag in Paris

Korean thinks: context first

저는 파리에서 가방을 샀어요

Cultural Context

Korean is deeply contextual. The same phrase can be polite, casual, or rude depending on the situation. I teach you to read the room, not just read the textbook.

7 Levels of "Thank you"

고마워 Close friends
감사해요 Polite
감사합니다 Formal
감사드립니다 Very formal
